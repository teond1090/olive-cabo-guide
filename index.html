The July Rhythm

July in Cabo has a rhythm — locals swear by it. Follow this and the heat never wins.

🌅 Mornings · 7–11 am

Glass-calm water and the best visibility. Book every boat, snorkel, and adventure tour in this window. The sea is at its absolute best before noon.

😎 Midday · 12–4 pm

Heat peaks near 91–96°F. This is pool time, shaded-lunch time, siesta time. Air conditioning is a Cabo activity. Drink more water than you think you need.

🌇 Evenings · 5 pm+

The town comes alive. Sunset cruises, marina strolls, dinner with toes in the sand. Quick July rain showers (if any) pass in under an hour.

🛟 Good-to-Knows

  • Swim where locals swim. Medano Beach & Chileno Bay are calm and swimmable; the Pacific-side beaches are beautiful but have strong currents — look for the flag system.
  • Drink bottled or filtered water. Resorts filter their ice; street ice is usually fine at established spots, but bottled is the easy rule.
  • Negotiate kindly. Water taxis and beach vendors expect a friendly haggle — start around 60–70% of the first price, with a smile.
  • July = baby sea-turtle season is coming. Nests start appearing on beaches — give marked nests space. 🐢
  • Emergency number is 911, same as home.
